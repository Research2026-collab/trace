Interactive Companions

Nine interactive pieces accompanying Shame as Resource in Indian Education: A Handbook for Teachers, Researchers, and Policy-Makers (Yadav, Vanderheiden & Varshney, 2026). Each companion accompanies a specific chapter and can be read on its own, without prior familiarity with the book.

Each companion below opens directly, no login or download required. Every page carries a narrow navigation strip at the top linking to all nine pieces, so you can move between them without returning here. The chapter number in each card refers to the corresponding section of the handbook.

Chapter 3.6

What Happens in the Brain During Shame?

Follows a single social-evaluative moment through the five-step neural cascade described in Chapter 3.6, from the amygdala's first alarm response to the attempt at cognitive regulation, with a schematic brain illustration highlighting the relevant structures at each step, and a final step showing where repeated activation leads.

Open companion

Chapter 4

Three Dimensions of Shame in Educational Settings

Reads a single classroom moment, a student unable to complete a derivation at the board, through the cognitive, performative and relational dimensions of shame developed in Chapter 4, before bringing all three together in the Triadic Self-Threat Model.

Open companion

Chapter 5

Cultural Shame Concepts

Presents four culturally grounded shame concepts from Indian philosophical, religious and social traditions, lajjā, vṛīḍā, apatrāpya and izzat, each unfolding through its own vignette, analytical commentary and a view of inhibiting and resource dimensions.

Open companion

Chapter 6

Body Map of Shame

Lets students mark directly on a body silhouette where they feel shame-related physical sensations, contributing to the empirical, embodied dimension of shame discussed in Chapter 6.

Open companion

Chapter 7

Capital Dimensions

Follows a single composite student through a design review session, reading the same situation through six capital dimensions developed in Chapter 7, cultural, educational, social, economic, linguistic and symbolic capital.

Open companion

Chapter 7.3

Teacher Interpersonal Behaviours

Translates the empirical teacher interpersonal behaviour model developed in Chapter 7.3 into an explorable form, tracing how supportive and shaming teaching behaviours shape recognition, warmth and belonging in Indian higher education.

Open companion

Chapter 10

Shame as Resource

Follows a single laboratory situation through five conditions developed in Chapter 10 under which shame can become a resource for learning rather than a lasting injury, public normalisation, active protection, private space, cognitive scaffolding and structural recognition.

Open companion

Chapter 12.1

Teaching in Shame-Sensitive Moments

Follows a single laboratory scene, in which a pharmacy student misjudges a calculation, hesitates before reporting her result, and briefly withdraws from asking for help, through eight tabs corresponding to the eight teaching moments discussed in Chapter 12.1, plus a ninth overview tab.

Open companion

Chapter 13.1

Caste as an Institutional Governance Problem

Anchors itself in a single documented finding, that at one university no higher-caste student chose a Dalit faculty member as doctoral supervisor, and reads this finding across the student, teaching staff and institutional levels at which caste operates as a governance problem.
